The Mark

The Log mark is the word "Log" in the product's monospace typeface, followed by a round period in the brand's amber accent. The period is the full stop — a thought completed, an entry committed to the record.

The period, not the cursor. A cursor signals anticipation. The period signals permanence. It is the stamp, the impression, the final character in every log entry.

The Idle Trace

Real recording instruments don't stop when you walk away. A seismograph keeps turning its drum. The idle trace is the gap in the record — it tells you the instrument was running. No guilt, no "welcome back," just the honest record of silence.

Seven Brand Principles

01
The brand is the surface, not a symbol applied to it.
The app itself is the brand expression. No external logo stamping. The timeline, the compose area, the idle trace — these are the brand.
02
The period, not the cursor.
Permanence over potential. The period says "the record is made." The cursor says "we're waiting." Log records; it doesn't wait.
03
Monochrome until it means something.
Color is signal, never decoration. The warm-neutral paper scale handles all structural needs. Color appears only when carrying semantic information.
04
Stillness over performance.
No decorative animation. No celebration. The instrument records faithfully. Motion exists only as mechanical feedback: shutter press, tape advance, mechanism reset.
05
The mechanism and the tape.
Monospace (Space Mono) is the mechanism: controls, labels, timestamps. Sans (IBM Plex Sans) is the tape: your words, what was recorded. This distinction teaches the system's philosophy through typography.
06
The drum turns between entries.
The idle trace records the gap honestly. No streak guilt. No "welcome back." The instrument was running. The tape just shows where you weren't writing.
07
The tape gets wider but the instrument stays the same.
New inputs (voice, images, locations) can be accepted. The recording mechanism — its feel, its colors, its rhythms — remains stable.
